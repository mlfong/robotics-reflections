In the week before spring break we went aerial and got dangerous. Parrot's AR Drone 2.0 Quadricopter. Wi-fi enabled with a crisp camera, this was a perfect opportunity to get a grasp on the computer vision side of robotics.

This assignment was split into three parts. The first was just to run the drone programmatically and plot it's angular and linear velocities. Fairly simple, nothing much different from our previous project with the Rovio.
Fig 1. Velocity plots

The second part was back to simulution. We did a bit of visual servoing where we took world points and turned them into camera points, with some green points as our target and our projections as red poitns. Our objective was to then move our camera so that our projections would match the targets.
Fig 2. Processing visual servoing simulation

In addition we tinkered with some of the possible visual servoing parameters such as our good old Kp component (PID anyone?) and varying distance approximations.
